Capture The Flag (CTF)

Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions are cybersecurity challenges where participants solve puzzles, exploit vulnerabilities, and gain experience in ethical hacking. CTFs simulate real-world scenarios, allowing players to learn topics like web security, reverse engineering, and cryptography.

FAQ

Question Answer
What is a CTF?A cybersecurity competition where players find hidden "flags" in challenges.
What are flags?Unique strings (e.g., CTF{example_flag}) that prove you solved a challenge.
What are common CTF categories?Web, Crypto, Forensics, Reverse Engineering, and Pwn.
What tools do players use?Burp Suite, Ghidra, Wireshark, Python scripts, and custom exploits.
Where can I play CTFs?Sites like Hack The Box, TryHackMe, and CTFtime list ongoing events.
What is a Jeopardy-style CTF?Players solve independent challenges worth varying points.
What is an Attack-Defense CTF?Teams defend their systems while attacking others’ services.
How do I start?Begin with beginner-friendly CTFs like picoCTF or TryHackMe labs.
Why are CTFs important?They teach practical cybersecurity skills and teamwork.
What skills do CTFs build?Problem-solving, scripting, system exploitation, and debugging.
